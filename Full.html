Sci-fi: 

			
			
 Ready Player One by Ernest Cline (Stand alone) 

			Five kids on a race to obtain cleverly hidden Easter eggs in a virtual world against a corrupt corporation who just wants money.
			
			

			
			
	 


			
			
 Warcross by Marie Lu (Series [uncomplete]) 

			A hacker, who instead of getting arrested after illegally getting into a world watched tournament, strikes a job with the richest.
			
			
  

			
			
	 


			
			
 H.I.V.E by Mark Walden (Series [uncomplete]) 

			A group of teen spies who are way smarter than the adults. Warning for violence.
			
			

			
			
	 


			
			
 Illuminae by Amie Kaufman and Jay Kristoff (Series [complete]) 

			Reading files just became 100 times more interesting (especially if they are about two teens and their journey through space while fighting off crazy bioweapon users and psychotic AIs.)
			
			

			
			
	 

		
			
Fiction(fantasy, teen, etc.): 


			
			
 Cinder by Marissa Meyer (Series [complete])

			Follow android Cinderella as she fights against insane moon people. Making fairy tales darker since 2012.
			
			

			
			
	 


			
			
 Throne of Glass by Sarah J. Mass (Series [uncomplete])

			An assassin who serves for a corrupt king and finds love(?) with the enemy(?).
			
			

			
			
	 


			
			
 Fire by Kristin Cashore (Series [complete])

			Assassin dealing with moral struggles and boys.
			
			

			
			
	 


			
			
 A Court of Thorns and Roses by Sarah J. Mass (Series [uncomplete])

			The small village girl enters the fairy world with a mountain of problems.
			
			

			
			
	 


			
			
 Alcatraz Versus the Evil Librarians by Brandon Sanderson (Series [complete])

			A boy with a talent of breaking things runs to save the world, starting with getting back his bag of sand.
			
			

			
			
	 

		
			
Contemporary:


			
			
 We Were Liars by Emily Jenkins (Stand alone)

			Four teens making very bad decisions and dealing with the consequences (well one of them is).
			
			

			
			
	 


			
			
 I'll Give You the Sun by Jandy Nelson (Stand alone)

			Twins during puberty finding different ways to express and on the way, finding themselves.
